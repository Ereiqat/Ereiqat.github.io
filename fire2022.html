PSUT Fire Fighter 2022

Overview

PSUT Fire Fighter Robotics Competition - First Place Victory

Introduction:

In July, 2023, I had the incredible opportunity to participate in the highly anticipated PSUT Fire Fighter robotics competition held at Princess Sumaya University for Technology (PSUT). As part of a talented and dedicated team, we named ourselves Double Face, and I took on the role of Team Leader. Our collective efforts, along with my individual contributions, led us to a resounding victory in the competition.

The Winning Team: Double Face

Our team, Double Face, was comprised of passionate robotics enthusiasts who shared a common goal: to design and build a robot that would excel in the simulated firefighting challenges. The members of our victorious team were:

  • Anas Ereiqat - Team Leader: As the team leader, I provided expert guidance and coordinated our efforts towards success.
  • Mohammad Doleh - Team Programmer: Mohammad's exceptional programming skills were instrumental in developing the robot's advanced autonomous navigation and strategic decision-making capabilities.
  • Ahmad Omari - Hardwear Designer: Ahmad's expertise in hardware design ensured our robot's sturdy build and efficient sensor integration.
  • Lafi Odeh - Strategies Designer: Lafi's strategic prowess enabled us to devise effective tactics to secure out win.

Robot Features:

Our robot, affectionately named "Dudi," was the culmination of our hard work and innovative ideas. Some of the key features that set our robot apart included:

  • Can quickly manouver in tight places to collect balls.
  • Has the ability to empty the balls in the tanks in such a short time
  • The ability to carry more than one animal simultaneously

Overcoming Challenges:

The journey to victory was not without its obstacles. We encountered several challenges during the preparation and competition stages. However, our team's resilience and problem-solving abilities enabled us to overcome these hurdles, such as:

  • We were limited in size
  • Having a lot of sensors, motors, and diffrent strategies make the code a bit more complex

Acknowledgments:

Our achievement would not have been possible without the support and guidance of various individuals and organizations. We extend our heartfelt gratitude to:

  • Eng. Youseg Jarbou & Eng. Omar Tobassi for their guidance and mentorship.

Conclusion:

The PSUT Fire Fighter robotics competition was an unforgettable experience that showcased our skills, determination, and passion for robotics. As we continue our journey in this exciting field, we look forward to exploring new challenges and making meaningful contributions to the world of robotics.
