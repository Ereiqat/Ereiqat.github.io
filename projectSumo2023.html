HTU Sumo Robotics 2023

Overview

Our Sumo Robot project aimed to create a powerful and agile robot capable of outmaneuvering opponents with strategic moves and sensor-based decision making. The robot was designed to be compact, sturdy, and packed with cutting-edge technology to gain a competitive advantage in the sumo ring.

The Winning Team: Double Face

Our team, Double Face, was comprised of passionate robotics enthusiasts who shared a common goal: to design and build a robot that would excel in the simulated firefighting challenges. The members of our victorious team were:

  • Anas Ereiqat - Team Leader: As the team leader and Hardware Designer, I played a crucial role in providing expert guidance and coordinating our efforts towards success. I was responsible for overseeing the mechanical aspects of the robot's design, ensuring its sturdiness and efficient sensor integration.
  • Mohammad Doleh - Team Programmer: Mohammad's exceptional programming skills were instrumental in developing the robot's advanced autonomous navigation and strategic decision-making capabilities. His expertise in coding and algorithm development contributed significantly to our robot's ability to outmaneuver opponents in the sumo ring.

Robot Features:

  • Autonomous Navigation: The robot used advanced algorithms and sensor feedback to autonomously navigate the sumo ring and detect the position of the opponent.
  • Precision Control: The robot was equipped with high-precision motors and controllers to execute precise movements and strategies during battles.
  • Sensor Suite: An array of sensors, including infrared and line sensors, were integrated to detect the opponent's position, track the arena boundaries, and avoid potential obstacles.
  • Intelligent Decision Making: The robot's control system utilized artificial intelligence to analyze sensor data and make strategic decisions during battles.
  • Sturdy Build: The robot's chassis was designed to withstand impacts and collisions, providing robustness during intense sumo matches.

The development of the Sumo Robot involved a series of phases:

  • Research and Planning: Extensive research was conducted to understand the rules, strategies, and components required for a successful sumo robot.
  • Design and Prototyping: Detailed mechanical and electrical designs were created, and prototypes were built and tested to fine-tune the robot's performance.
  • Programming: The robot's control system was programmed in C using Arduino Uno bpard, implementing algorithms for navigation, decision making, and opponent detection.
  • Testing and Iteration: The robot underwent rigorous testing and iterative improvements to enhance its performance and adaptability.

Competition Success:

At the 2023 HTU Sumo Robot Competition, our robot performed exceptionally well, securing the 3rd place best strategy award among a talented pool of competitors. The robot's ability to swiftly identify opponents, respond to their movements, and maintain its position in the ring was key to its success.

Lessons Learned:

This project was not only a technical challenge but also a valuable learning experience. Through this journey, we gained expertise in robotics, sensor integration, artificial intelligence, and precision control systems. Additionally, I developed skills in leadership ,teamwork, problem-solving, and adapting to the dynamic nature of competitive robotics.
